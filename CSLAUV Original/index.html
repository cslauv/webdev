About Us

The goal of CSLA AUV is to design, build, and test autonomous underwater vehicles to compete in the annual AUVSI RoboSub Competition. The AUV will traverse an obstacle course underwater and perform a series of pre-determined tasks without any human interaction.

Our team consists of a wide variety of students in both experience and academic disciplines. The team is open to undergraduates, graduate students, and even CSLA alumni. The team is heavily multidisciplinary, including students from mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, and business. These students are organized into various sub-teams, and are then given responsibilities that allow them to learn and excel.

  • Divisions

    The CSLAUV research club is made up of three divisions:

    • Mechanical Division:

    We are the body of CSLA AUV. Our mission is to bring ideas for auv to life, using material resources to build our one of a kind auv.

    • Frame/Concealment

    The objective of this group is to design and build the frame that supports electrical components. The group uses SolidWorks as the main application to design the intended frame. Together with the Electrical Division, both teams collaborate to conceal electrical components making the AUV an underwater vehicle.

      Propulsion

    This group focuses on the components that make the AUV move. The team decides which trusters are suitible for the AUV and along with the design for the missle marker. The design objective for the missle marker is for the AUV to launch a small torpedo onto a target.

      • Electrical Division:

    Think of us as the nutrient of CSLA AUV. We are the connection that keep the body (mechanic) and mind (software) alive.

    • Controls

    The group collaborates with the Computer Science Divison team to design the controls to control the AUV.

      Power

    The group focuses on how much voltage each component receives. The group also oversees the wiring task making sure all components are correctly wired to its appropriate part.

      • Computer Science Division:

    We are the networks and brain of CSLA AUV. Our humble desire is to bring our auv to life with full functionality that allows it to respond to its environment. Our team also showcase what happens in the background of CSLA AUV to people via the world wide web.

    • Computer Vision

    The Comptuer Vision team decides which sensors to use for the AUV. This enables the autonomous AUV to scan its environment to avoid obstacles. The group works with the Electrical Divison making sure all components work properly.

      Simulation Testing

    The team tests all programming components corresponds to the inteded output. The group simulates environments for the AUV to test before components are physcially installed onto the AUV.

      Web Design

        The group designs the team's website. The purpose of the website is to display information about the team for the interested to view.
